Peruvian Ceviche

Description

Peruvian Ceviche is one of the most representative dishes of Peruvian gastronomy and a cultural heritage of the nation. It consists of fresh fish marianated in lime juice, which "cooks" the fish with its acidity. It's traditionally accompannied by red onion, chilli peppers, cilantro, and served with swet potato and corn, creating a refreshing experience full of intense flavors.

Ingredients

  • 500g fresh white fish (sea bass or sole)
  • 8 Peruvian limes (or 12 regular limes)
  • 1 medium red onion
  • 2 yellow chili peppers
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Salt to taste
  • Ground black pepper
  • 1 cooked sweet potato
  • 1 cooked corn on the cob
  • Lettuce leaves for serving
  • Cancha serrana (toasted corn)

Steps

  1. Cut the fish into apporximately 2cm cubes and place in a bowl
  2. Squeeze the limes until you have enough juice to cover the fish
  3. Cut the red onion into fine julienne strips and soak in cold salted water for 10 minutes
  4. DEvein in the yellow chilo peppers, remove seeds and cut into thin strips
  5. Cursh the garlic clove with a little salt until it forms a paste
  6. Drain the onion and mix it with the fish
  7. Add the crushed garlic, chili peppers and season with salt and pepper
  8. Pour the lime juice over the fish and mix gently
  9. Let marinate in the refrigerator for 10 to 15 minutes until the fish is "cooked"
  10. Serve immediatly garnished with cilantro, accompanied by sweet potato, corn and cancha serrana on lettuce leaves
